## Appendix A — Security Services Addendum

This Addendum applies to any SOW involving security assessment, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, or red team services. It supplements the MSA and prevails over the MSA to the extent of any conflict.

### A.1 Rules of Engagement (Required Before Start)

Before any scanning, testing, or reconnaissance begins, Client must provide MAS-AI a signed Rules of Engagement document stating:

- (a) **In-scope targets:** Specific domains, IP ranges, CIDRs, repositories, mobile app package identifiers, or API endpoints. No implicit expansion.
- (b) **Out-of-scope targets:** Any systems, subdomains, or environments Client does not authorize MAS-AI to touch.
- (c) **Testing window:** Start date, end date, and any blackout hours (e.g., no testing during peak load).
- (d) **Permitted techniques:** Whether denial-of-service, social engineering, physical testing, or destructive tests are authorized (default: none).
- (e) **Emergency contact:** A 24/7 reachable person at Client for incidents during testing.

### A.2 No Guarantee of Complete Discovery

Client specifically acknowledges that Section 6.1 of the MSA applies with full force to security engagements. **No security assessment is complete.** MAS-AI uses industry-leading tooling (including Klyntar with 25+ exploit signatures, Zero-FP gating, BeyondMythos enrichment, and frontier-level discovery) but cannot guarantee the identification of all vulnerabilities, and cannot prevent future attacks or zero-day exploitation.

### A.3 Data Handling

- (a) MAS-AI will not exfiltrate, retain, or transfer any Client Data beyond what is strictly necessary for the engagement.
- (b) All credentials, session tokens, or sensitive material pulled into Klyntar during the engagement are destroyed within 24 hours of final delivery.
- (c) MAS-AI will not publicly disclose any Client finding without Client's prior written consent.
- (d) MAS-AI may aggregate anonymized metrics (e.g., "we find X vulnerabilities per engagement on average") for internal improvement and public-facing case studies, with no Client-identifying detail.

### A.4 Proof-of-Exploit Standard

For findings rated OPERATOR tier or above, MAS-AI commits to including a reproducible proof-of-exploit. Findings that cannot be reproduced under the Zero-FP gate are dropped from the report. This is a deliverable standard, not a warranty of completeness.

### A.5 Remediation Support

Remediation advice included in the audit report is guidance, not a warranty. MAS-AI may offer separate paid remediation architecture as a follow-on SOW at Client's option.

### A.6 Regulatory Disclosure

If during the engagement MAS-AI discovers evidence of a prior or active breach, unauthorized access, or regulated-data exposure, MAS-AI will (a) stop testing on the affected target, (b) notify Client's emergency contact within 4 business hours, and (c) cooperate with Client's counsel and incident response. Client is responsible for any regulatory disclosure obligations under applicable law (e.g., GDPR, PIPEDA, CCPA, HIPAA, EU AI Act).

### A.7 Liability Cap for Security Services

For security engagements, the liability cap in Section 6.3 of the MSA is **the fees paid under the specific security SOW**, not the 12-month aggregate. This lower cap reflects the higher-risk nature of security work and is an essential basis of the bargain.
